How We Remember The Web

These are the stories of our first memories of when networked computers touched our lives. It’s good to realize that this thing that’s been a part of life for so long is the same thing we can take part in building today

.

Joshua

I’d loved computers for a long time, ever since I was introduced to them in grade school.
Classrooms had Old Macs in them where you could do dot-matrix printing and play Oregon Trail. We used them to draw and to learn. I stayed fascinated for years, but it was one day when I was 12 years old that my neighbor friend told me they got the internet. I had heard about the internet, that it connected you to information and that you could find pages about anything you could think of. I called him up and asked if I could come over. When I got there we ran straight upstairs to the office and sat down. He connected and the modem made all its weird sounds and then he opened up Netscape and I watched the first webpage load in slowly. Yahoo. There was the funny logo with the exclamation point. The list of blue links. There were categories for all kinds of things. We spent the next 4 hours clicking from category to category and from page to page, seeing what kinds of wild sites we could find. That evening I started my campaign of annoying my parents to death to get a computer for our home and it had to be one that could connect to the internet.

.

Jana

I can remember quite well the first time that I used the web since I was actually waiting for finally "getting installed the internet” on my computer. It was in 2000 and I had to dial in via a modem (I’m old enough to remember the pre-flat rate time) and I couldn’t wait to create an E-Mail account for being able to send my very first E-Mail to the only other one of my classmates that had Internet already at this time. Funnily this new way of communication & connecting people that the Internet provided was the most exciting purpose for me and shortly after we discovered the world of chatrooms and were totally amazed of the possibility to talk to people somewhere in real-time.

Andres

So the first time I actually felt the internet was impactful of my life was back in the day was, before school introduced us to a computer showing us how to do reading AR test and some history games but it didn't mean much to me. So one day my friend comes to my house and he shows me a game called Runescape back in 2008 and I was curious to see what the game was. It was an MMORPG that opened the world to me to online chatting, gaming, sense of achievement when leveling or powering up my character and even meeting new friends. With that I was hooked then later he showed me how youtube was and how I could stream online videos on youtube. After some time I was so into Runescape I downloaded a Hypercam 2 screen recorder and started filming my youtube tutorials for power-up in the games and downloading video software to edit the video and uploading it , later I found private servers and I downloaded one and started to edit the code to make me owner of the server and that's how I got into coding. Also from the MMORPG genre started to introduce me to other games like WOW and later future games and new communities of people I have met online, youtube as well with the videos and then looking up coding tutorials and other places to grab information.
