Interactions with the web

Remininscing on how we were introduced to the internet and what impact it has on us.

There are still people around who remember a world before the internet. A world where our lives were not intertwined with technology.

Joshua

I’d loved computers for a long time, ever since I was introduced to them in grade school. Classrooms had old Macs in them where you could do dot-matrix printing and play Oregon Trail. We used them to draw and to learn. I stayed fascinated for years, but it was one day when I was 12 years old that my neighbor friend told me they got the internet. I had heard about the internet, that it connected you to information and that you could find pages about anything you could think of. I called him up and asked if I could come over. When I got there we ran straight upstairs to the office and sat down. He connected and the modem made all its weird sounds and then he opened up Netscape and I watched the first webpage load in slowly. Yahoo. There was the funny logo with the exclamation point. The list of blue links. There were categories for all kinds of things. We spent the next 4 hours clicking from category to category and from page to page, seeing what kinds of wild sites we could find. That evening I started my campaign of annoying my parents to death to get a computer for our home and it had to be one that could connect to the internet.


Daniela

I was fourth grade and received an assignment to write a paper on our national poet. Until then there was quite a rule on only using books and going to the almost inexistent library for school work. A classmate who made a comprehensive paper told me she found the info on Google. So I go home and tell my parents to let me use the internet for school It was frustrating because it is about a Romanian poet, and the information is scarce so it was hard to find what I was looking for. Furthermore, I always found the modem to be incredibly unaesthetic. I never shared that thought though because I thought the benefit of having internet access outweighed the visual handicap. I considerably improved my English by reading tech and science articles on Yahoo starting with the age of 10-11. I read more than a handful a day and remember at that time I wanted to design devices myself.


Oliva

I don’t remember any remarkable first interactions with the web, but I remember a time in my childhood where it was all about Bluetooth. We exchanged ringtones, cool background pictures, etc. I always got so frustrated when my Bluetooth didn’t work, which was almost always. But I got such joy when it worked and I could use this new ringtone as my own as well or the new background wallpaper or just listen to the song that I received. This feeling of being able to share something with your friends was so amazing at the time. Most of the things I did on a computer were offline in the beginning as far as I can remember.


Alina

To be honest I don't remember the first time I used the internet because as I remember it always was there. Though, I remember some funny stories about how I was learning to understand what is it and how it works. So I am going to write about one of them. As I already mentioned, the Internet was existing since I was born, and when I was 5 years old I remember that my family had one super old computer, which looked like a big plastic box, and a more new version of computer, which was already quite flat. Everyone, of course, was using the new one because it was faster and better, and I, as the smallest person in the family was left to play with an old one. Apparently, the new computer was connected to the internet and I was observing my parents searching web, so I knew everything about how it looked inside and how to use it. Well, at least I thought that I knew everything. And one day, playing around the old computer I found the icon of Internet Explorer and I was sure that this icon means that I can access the internet. And being very excited about my discovery I decided to search for something. When I clicked on it nothing happened because the computer was not connected to any network but for a moment I thought that I broke something in the computer because I was thinking that you can install the "Internet" on your computer like all the other programs. I came to my parents with this story and they explained to me that the internet is not just one program.



What about me?

I'm not sure if this was the first time I interacted with the web but what I do remember was email using AOL. My brother and I were still really young (around 6) and we would draw a lot. My father lived in Switzerland while we were in Germany, so we often would fax our drawings. But that meant that my father always saw them in greyscale. Then we got an email account where we could scan and send our drawings to my father. He finally got to see them in color and we could respond to our "artwork". I remember being amazed by this and we were always very excited to check our email to see any comments (Mum always supervised us, she wasn't very trusting of the web!)
