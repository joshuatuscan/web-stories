Daniela

First time I browsed the web

I was fourth grade and received an assignment to write a paper on our national poet. Until then there was quite a rule on only using books and going to the almost inexistent library for school work. A classmate who made a comprehensive paper told me she found the info on Google. So I go home and tell my parents to let me use the internet for school It was frustrating because it is about a Romanian poet, and the information is scarce so it was hard to find what I was looking for. Furthermore, I always found the modem to be incredibly unaesthetic. I never shared that thought though because I thought the benefit of having internet access outweighed the visual handicap.

Early impact of the internet

I considerably improved my English by reading tech and science articles on Yahoo starting with the age of 10-11. I read more than a handful a day and remember at that time I wanted to design devices myself.




Joshua

First time I browsed the web

I'd loved computers for a long time, ever since I was introduced to them in grade school. Classrooms had old Macs in them where you could do dot-matrix printing and play Oregon Trail. We used them to draw and to learn. I stayed fascinated for years, but it was one day when I was 12 years old that my neighbor friend told me they got the internet. I had heard about the internet, that it connected you to information and that you could find pages about anything you could think of. I called him up and asked if I could come over. When I got there we ran straight upstairs to the office and sat down. He connected and the modem made all its weird sounds and then he opened up Netscape and I watched the first webpage load in slowly. Yahoo. There was the funny logo with the exclamation point. The list of blue links. There were categories for all kinds of things. We spent the next 4 hours clicking from category to category and from page to page, seeing what kinds of wild sites we could find. That evening I started my campaign of annoying my parents to death to get a computer for our home and it had to be one that could connect to the internet.

Victor

My first experience using the internet

My first experience using the internet is most likely a relatable one. I was in school, in Paris, and the teacher brought the entire class up to the computer room. I can't remember if the computers were just installed or if it was just the first time we where allowed to use them but I do recall that one particular student had some previous knowledge, mainly due to the fact that he knew how to bypass the school block and was able to get us straight to the most exciting games website. I remember the walls were orange but I have not the recollection of the computer we where using or what we were supposed to be doing (most likely something less exciting like WolframAlpha or paint). Once we got onto the games website I was blown away by the number of games I could play at my will, it was like an endless supply of fun to me and I was incredibly excited. It was clear at that moment that I had underestimated the internet.
My mother had a Mac in the house which she mainly used to write, and I'm sure we had an internet connection at that time but I had never tried using it. To me, the computer was just a tool for work and there was nothing cool or desirable about that, I had no interest in them whatsoever. This obviously changed when I found all the games. Shooting aliens or just simple platformersâ€¦ I was hooked.
So that's my first real memory of using one and being excited by one. First, the thrill of being able to bypass the school block on forbidden websites then finding out that there where entire websites filled with thousands of games, my mind was blown.




Leah

I'm not sure if this was the first time

I'm not sure if this was the first time I interacted with the web but what I do remember was email using AOL. My brother and I were still really young (around 6) and we would draw a lot. My father lived in Switzerland while we were in Germany, so we often would fax our drawings. But that meant that my father always saw them in greyscale. Then we got an email account where we could scan and send our drawings to my father. He finally got to see them in color and we could respond to our "artwork". Alina

I thought you can instal your internet

To be honest I don't remember the first time I used the internet because as I remember it always was there. Though, I remember some funny stories about how I was learning to understand what is it and how it works. So I am going to write about one of them.
As I already mentioned, the Internet was existing since I was born, and when I was 5 years old I remember that my family had one super old computer, which looked like a big plastic box, and a more new version of computer, which was already quite flat. Everyone, of course, was using the new one because it was faster and better, and I, as the smallest person in the family was left to play with an old one. Apparently, the new computer was connected to the internet and I was observing my parents searching web, so I knew everything about how it looked inside and how to use it. Well, at least I thought that I knew everything. And one day, playing around the old computer I found the icon of Internet Explorer and I was sure that this icon means that I can access the internet. And being very excited about my discovery I decided to search for something. When I clicked on it nothing happened because the computer was not connected to any network but for a moment I thought that I broke something in the computer because I was thinking that you can install the "Internet" on your computer like all the other programs. I came to my parents with this story and they explained to me that the internet is not just one program.




Wei

I believe my first interaction with the web was in my father's office

To be honest I don't remember the first time I used the internet because as I remember it always was there. Though, I remember some funny stories about how I was learning to understand what is it and how it works. So I am going to write about one of them.
I believe my first interaction with the web was in my father's office when they first installed a computer with windows95. I believe when the browser opened it was empty and then we would go to websites like sina.com , yahoo.com or sohu.com , which were the main digital publisher at the time (of course I had no idea what "digital publisher" meant at the time). I remember the "minimize" and "close" button on the top left (or right?) Was quite confusing, and my father was freaked out when he saw the window disappeared after I clicked on "_". He would think I "broke" the computer.
It was all text on the webpage, sight a few images, which we would click with the mouse to get to the content. It was kind of like reading newspapers and magazines, just with a bit more contents. The navigation was a bit confusing (or computers in general), that we would close the browser and reopen it to get to the content on the same website if we were "lost" and couldn't find our way back to the main page. Back button, refresh button were magic to us and we were so scared just to use them too often, feeling it was like reusing a physical object which would eventually wear out and the computer will break down.
Internet Explorer was, of course, the only way of browsing. The blue "e" icon was like a window to the world, center of the action, which magic happens when double clicked on.
